<objective>
Close the SSE same-tick subscription-teardown gap (BUG-04). The audit in 103-RESEARCH.md §"SSE Race Audit" confirms a real gap: `reconnectDueToTimeout()` (stream-service.registry.ts 142-164) closes and nulls `_currentEventSource` and schedules a reconnect, but it does NOT unsubscribe `_currentSubscription`. The subscription stays live for the 3000ms retry window; if the just-closed EventSource fires `onerror` in that window (some implementations fire onerror on `.close()`), the subscription's `error` handler (243-260) schedules a SECOND `createSseObserver`, producing two reconnects / two EventSources. The fix is the minimal two-line teardown — `this._currentSubscription?.unsubscribe(); this._currentSubscription = null;` — at the TOP of `reconnectDueToTimeout()`, mirroring the identical entry teardown already present at `createSseObserver()` (181-182). This guarantees exactly one active subscription/EventSource after a same-tick timeout+error collision.

Purpose: BUG-04 prevents orphaned SSE subscriptions and duplicate EventSources (availability/correctness; Tampering/Information-Disclosure risk from a stale orphaned listener double-delivering events).

Output: `stream-service.registry.ts` with the leading teardown in `reconnectDueToTimeout()`; a new `describe("BUG-04 same-tick reconnect collision")` regression test in `stream-service.registry.spec.ts` asserting exactly one reconnect; the slice-1 heartbeat-vs-timeout race suite preserved.
</objective>
