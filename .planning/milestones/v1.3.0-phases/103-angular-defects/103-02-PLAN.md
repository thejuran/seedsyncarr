<objective>
Close the SSE same-tick subscription-teardown gap (BUG-04). The audit in 103-RESEARCH.md ┬ž"SSE Race Audit" confirms a real gap: `reconnectDueToTimeout()` (stream-service.registry.ts 142-164) closes and nulls `_currentEventSource` and schedules a reconnect, but it does NOT unsubscribe `_currentSubscription`. The subscription stays live for the 3000ms retry window; if the just-closed EventSource fires `onerror` in that window (some implementations fire onerror on `.close()`), the subscription's `error` handler (243-260) runs against a torn-down connection ÔÇö it delivers a SECOND `notifyDisconnected()` to every registered service and clears-then-re-arms the reconnect timer. The fix is the minimal two-line teardown ÔÇö `this._currentSubscription?.unsubscribe(); this._currentSubscription = null;` ÔÇö at the TOP of `reconnectDueToTimeout()`, mirroring the identical entry teardown already present at `createSseObserver()` (181-182). This guarantees that after a same-tick timeout+error collision the stale subscription is already closed, so its error handler is a no-op (RxJS swallows `error` on an unsubscribed subscriber): no duplicate disconnect, exactly one active subscription/EventSource.

Purpose: BUG-04 prevents orphaned SSE subscriptions and duplicate disconnect notifications (availability/correctness; Tampering/Information-Disclosure risk from a stale orphaned listener double-delivering events).

Output: `stream-service.registry.ts` with the leading teardown in `reconnectDueToTimeout()`; a new `describe("BUG-04 same-tick reconnect collision")` regression test in `stream-service.registry.spec.ts` whose RED discriminator is the duplicate disconnect side-effect (a second `false` in `connectedSeq`); the slice-1 heartbeat-vs-timeout race suite preserved.
</objective>
